**Policy Strategies**

* **Expand access to supportive care services.** This includes services such as physical therapy, occupational therapy, nutrition counseling, and mental health counseling. These services can help cancer survivors manage their symptoms, improve their quality of life, and return to their normal activities.
* **Provide financial assistance to cancer survivors.** This could include assistance with medical bills, transportation costs, and childcare expenses. Financial assistance can help cancer survivors focus on their health and well-being without having to worry about their financial situation.
* **Encourage employers to be more accommodating to cancer survivors.** This could include providing flexible work arrangements, allowing for telecommuting, and offering paid sick leave. Cancer survivors often need to miss work for doctor's appointments, treatment, and recovery. Flexible work arrangements can help them stay employed and maintain their income.
* **Increase public awareness of the long-term effects of cancer.** Many cancer survivors experience long-term side effects from their treatment, such as fatigue, pain, and cognitive impairment. Public awareness of these side effects can help cancer survivors get the support they need to manage their symptoms and improve their quality of life.

**Research Strategies**

* **Invest in research to develop new treatments that have fewer side effects.** This would help cancer survivors reduce their risk of long-term side effects and improve their quality of life.
* **Invest in research to develop better methods for managing the long-term side effects of cancer.** This would help cancer survivors manage their symptoms and improve their quality of life.
* **Invest in research to develop better methods for identifying and preventing cancer recurrence.** This would help cancer survivors live longer and healthier lives.

These are just a few of the policy and research strategies that could be implemented to improve the quality of life of cancer survivors. By taking these steps, we can help cancer survivors live long, healthy, and fulfilling lives.

In addition to these strategies, it is also important to provide support to cancer survivors and their families. This could include emotional support, financial support, and practical assistance. There are many organizations that provide support to cancer survivors, such as the American Cancer Society, the National Cancer Institute, and the Leukemia & Lymphoma Society. These organizations can provide information, resources, and support to help cancer survivors cope with the challenges of cancer and improve their quality of life.
