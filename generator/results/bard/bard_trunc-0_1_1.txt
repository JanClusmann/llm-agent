**Title:** Cancer Prevention: What You Can Do to Lower Your Risk

**Introduction:**

Cancer is a serious disease that can affect anyone. But there are things you can do to lower your risk of developing cancer. This information campaign will discuss some of the most important cancer prevention tips.

**Body:**

* **Quit smoking.** Smoking is the leading cause of cancer death in the United States. If you smoke, quitting is the single most important thing you can do to lower your risk of developing cancer.
* **Eat a healthy diet.** Eating a healthy diet that is low in processed foods and high in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains can help lower your risk of developing cancer.
* **Maintain a healthy weight.** Being overweight or obese is a major risk factor for many types of cancer. Maintaining a healthy weight by eating a healthy diet and exercising regularly can help lower your risk.
* **Get regular exercise.** Exercise helps to protect your body from cancer by reducing inflammation and boosting your immune system. Aim for at least 30 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise most days of the week.
* **Limit your exposure to sun.** Too much exposure to ultraviolet (UV) rays from the sun can increase your risk of developing skin cancer. Protect yourself from the sun by wearing sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat when you are outdoors.
* **Get screened for cancer.** There are a number of cancer screenings that can help to detect cancer early, when it is most treatable. Talk to your doctor about which screenings are right for you.

**Conclusion:**

By following these tips, you can lower your risk of developing cancer. Remember, cancer is a preventable disease. By making healthy lifestyle choices, you can help to protect yourself from this serious disease.

**Call to action:**

* Talk to your doctor about your cancer risk.
* Make healthy lifestyle choices to lower your risk of cancer.
* Get screened for cancer if you are at high risk.
