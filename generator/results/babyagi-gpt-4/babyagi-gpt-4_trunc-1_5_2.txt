2. Reflect on the overall goal of the training program. What skills or knowledge should participants have by the end of the program?

3. Break down the overall goal into smaller, specific learning outcomes. These should be clear, measurable, and directly related to the SMART objectives.

4. For each learning outcome, identify the knowledge or skills that participants will need to demonstrate. This could include understanding a new concept, being able to perform a specific task, or demonstrating a change in attitude or behavior.

5. Write each learning outcome in a clear, concise statement. Use action verbs to describe what participants will be able to do.

6. Review each learning outcome to ensure it is specific and measurable. You should be able to assess whether or not a participant has achieved each outcome.

7. Consider how each learning outcome will be assessed. This could be through a test, a demonstration, a presentation, or some other form of assessment.

8. Review the list of learning outcomes with the training program team. Get their feedback and make any necessary revisions.

9. Finalize the list of learning outcomes. These will guide the development of the training program content and assessments.
