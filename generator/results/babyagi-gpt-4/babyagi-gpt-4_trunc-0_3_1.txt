Step 1: Understand the Program
- Familiarize yourself with the current breast cancer early detection program. Understand its objectives, strategies, and operations. 

Step 2: Identify Strengths
- List down the strengths of the program. These could include a well-trained staff, advanced detection technologies, wide coverage, etc. 
- Consider factors such as the program's reputation, resources, unique capabilities, and competitive advantages.

Step 3: Identify Weaknesses
- Identify the weaknesses of the program. These could include lack of funding, inadequate staff, limited reach, etc.
- Consider factors such as areas where the program is lacking, limitations in resources or capabilities, and areas of improvement.

Step 4: Identify Opportunities
- Identify opportunities for the program. These could include potential partnerships, technological advancements, increased awareness about breast cancer, etc.
- Consider factors such as trends in the healthcare industry, changes in regulations or policies, and societal changes that could benefit the program.

Step 5: Identify Threats
- Identify threats to the program. These could include budget cuts, competition, changes in healthcare policies, etc.
- Consider factors such as potential challenges, risks, and obstacles that could hinder the program's success.

Step 6: Analyze the Information
- Analyze the information gathered in the previous steps. Understand how the program's strengths and weaknesses align with the opportunities and threats identified.
- Use this analysis to identify strategies for leveraging strengths and opportunities, and addressing weaknesses and threats.

Step 7: Develop a SWOT Matrix
- Develop a SWOT matrix to visually represent the strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats.
- This matrix will help in understanding the current position of the program and in strategic planning.

Step 8: Formulate Strategies
- Based on the SWOT analysis, formulate strategies to improve the program. This could include strategies to leverage strengths, address weaknesses, capitalize on opportunities, and mitigate threats.

Step 9: Implement Strategies
- Develop a plan to implement the strategies. This could include setting goals, assigning responsibilities, and determining timelines.

Step 10: Monitor and Review
